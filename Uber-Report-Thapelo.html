Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Univariate and Bivariate Analysis

  1. Distribution of Pickups (Univariate)
library(ggplot2)
# Histogram for number of pickups
ggplot(clean_uber_data, aes(x = pickups)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 50, fill = "steelblue", color = "white") +
  labs(title = "Distribution of Pickups", x = "Number of Pickups", y = "Frequency")

Observation: The distribution of Uber pickups it is left-skewed, this state that the majority of recorded time intervals have an unusually high number of pickups, with the median and mode of the data being closer to the maximum possible pickup count. There are very few instances (the tail on the left) where the number of pickups is very low.

  1. Average Pickups by Hour (Time Series)
# Average pickups by hour
pickups_by_hour <- clean_uber_data %>%
  group_by(hour) %>%
  summarize(avg_pickups = mean(pickups))

ggplot(pickups_by_hour, aes(x = hour, y = avg_pickups, group = 1)) +
  geom_line(color = "red") +
  geom_point(color = "red", size = 2) +
  labs(title = "Average Pickups by Hour of Day", x = "Hour of Day", y = "Average Pickups")

Observation: Pickup demand is low during the early morning hours (00:00 to 06:00), steadily increases throughout the day, and peaks during the evening commute/late night (around 17:00 to 20:00).

3.Total Pickups by Borough (Geographical)

# Total pickups by borough
pickups_by_borough <- clean_uber_data %>%
  group_by(borough) %>%
  summarize(total_pickups = sum(pickups))

ggplot(pickups_by_borough, aes(x = reorder(borough, total_pickups), y = total_pickups)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", fill = "skyblue") +
  labs(title = "Total Pickups by Borough", x = "Borough", y = "Total Pickups") +
  coord_flip()

Observation: Manhattan is the borough with the highest total number of Uber pickups by a significant margin, followed by Brooklyn and Queens. This is expected given Manhattan’s density and concentration of business and entertainment.

Conclusion This exploratory analysis has revealed several key insights into Uber pickup patterns:

Temporal Trends: Pickup demand exhibits a strong daily cycle, peaking between 5 PM and 8 PM.

Geographical Demand: Manhattan is the dominant source of pickups.

Weather Impact: Pickups appear to be marginally higher during rainy hours and show a weak positive association with temperature.

Holiday Effect: Pickup activity is generally lower on holidays compared to non-holidays.

These findings can be crucial for Uber’s operational planning, driver positioning, and dynamic pricing strategies.
